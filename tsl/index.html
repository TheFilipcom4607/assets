Why Swifties switch

Every subsystem rewritten from scratch, because we believe in owning our own masters.

📦

swiftie package manager

Install anything with swiftie get folklore. Dependency conflicts are resolved by writing a bridge. Broken packages get re-recorded as (taylors-version).

🐚

The Eras Shell (esh)

Your prompt changes theme every era. cd into a directory you left years ago and esh whispers "welcome back, it's been a long time coming."

🔁

(Taylor's Version) snapshots

Don't like who owns your current system state? Re-record it. tsl-snapshot rerecord / gives you back full ownership, with four bonus files From The Vault.

🕛

Midnights cron

All scheduled jobs run at midnight by default, because that's when the real work gets done. Meet the maintainers at midnight.

🧣

All Too Well journaling FS

A filesystem that remembers everything, in extended detail. The 10-minute fsck is longer, sadder, and objectively better than the original.

🐍

reputation security module

Look what you made us do: mandatory access control that trusts nobody. Old vulnerabilities can't come to the phone right now. Why? Oh — 'cause they're patched.

💔

Graceful breakups

Uninstalling a package prints we are never ever getting back together and actually removes the config files. Unlike some distros. And some exes.

✨

Bejeweled compositor

Your desktop can still make the whole screen shimmer. 120 fps of polish, even on that laptop from the Speak Now era.

🚪

exile containers

Misbehaving processes are sent to exile — a namespace so isolated they'll think they've seen this sandbox before. Duet-mode requires a second maintainer.
